Martine Hughes - Interview

-

1. How did you get interested in motocross? My father drove a bit when he was little, and bought me a ktm 50cc when I was 4 years old. Been driving since then.


2. How do you train to stay in shape and improve your skills? Apart from driving, I do a lot of endurance and strength training. The strength training is often body weight training, so as not to build too many muscles and to become flexible.


3. What is the biggest challenge you have faced as a motocross athlete? The biggest challenge I have faced as a motocross athlete is when I lived in France. There were both positive and negative things. What made it difficult was living alone so far and for so long away from family and friends. Lonely.


4. How has your family reacted to you choosing to become a motocross athlete? Some are supportive while others don't think so.


5. What is the most memorable experience you have had as a motocross athlete? I've had many. But I particularly remember a race when I raced 85cc against the boys at a Westland championship. Not a big race, but I won that weekend, and I remember it very well.


6. How do you prepare mentally before a race? I am always mentally prepared when I know that I have trained hard and smart before a race.


7. What are your long-term goals and ambitions as a motocross athlete? My goal is to become world champion.


8. How do you think the sport of motocross will develop in the future? Haven't thought much about it.


9. What would you say to other young girls who want to become motocross athletes? Have fun and do it because you want to.


10. Who is your biggest inspiration in motocross? I had Magne Klingsheim and Genette Våge as role models when I was little. But I don't have anything special I'm looking up to so far.
